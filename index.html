New Zealanders love cats, and cats would be pleased to know they are the most popular pet in our nation with 45% of Kiwis having a feline friend. But we also love our native birds too. As cat owners know, domestic cats can be highly efficient killers.

Native Birds

Kiwi

It is the species that lives closest to human habitation. It is also the main species on display in captivity.  This proximity of people to kiwi has created its own risks to birds through increased contact with dogs, cats and cars.

 

Fantail

It is easily recognised by its long tail which opens to a fan. It has a small head and bill and has two colour forms, pied and melanistic or black. The pied birds are grey-brown with white and black bands.

Tui

They are adaptable birds, and can also be found in suburban areas, particularly in winter if there is a flowering gum about.

These attractive birds can often be heard singing their beautiful melodies long before they are spotted.

If you are fortunate to glimpse one you will recognise them by their distinctive white tuft under their throat. This tuft contrasts dramatically with the metallic blue-green sheen to their underlying black colour.

Until the arrival of humans, about 1000 years ago, there were no four legged predators here. Our native birds had evolved over millions of years without the inbuilt instinct to avoid them.

Cat Control

Bells

Studies have found that placing bells on cats halved the number of birds caught.

The Dunedin research was carried out on 37 cats who were Dunedin’s most prolific hunters. The cats caught 82 birds without collars, but only 41 birds were caught when the cats wore bells.
